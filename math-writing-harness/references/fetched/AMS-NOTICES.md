# AMS-NOTICES — AMS Notices Author Guidelines (fetched 2026-07-08 from ams.org/publications/notices/authorguidelines; full page read)

Venue-calibration source: how a broad-audience mathematical venue defines its expectations. Useful as the "expository register" pole of the L5 venue axis (research journals = AMS-HB/AMS-STYLE pole).

## General guidelines (all article types; "many of these suggestions can be fruitfully applied to all mathematical writing")
1. "You are writing for the general AMS membership … Your audience is not colleagues who work in your field. Hook the non-specialists."
2. The first few paragraphs must answer, for readers who stop there: What is your article about? Where is your subject coming from, where is it now, where might it be going? Why is your topic interesting and exciting? — and convince the rest to continue.
3. **"The majority of your readers will not read to the end, so do not hide your punchline there. Think more 'newspaper article' and less 'murder mystery.' Describe the sizzle in the introduction, even if details only come later."**
4. Key principles: Know what you want to say. Understand your audience. Think and write clearly. Repeatedly read and polish. **"Many people find that reading aloud helps improve readability."**
5. No field-jargon that isn't standard for the general mathematical community.
6. **Banned register moves: "Recall that X" / "It is well known that X"** — "they signal to the reader who has never run across X that there is little hope of understanding the rest" — **and "Clearly X is true"** — "if X is not immediately clear to the reader, then you've lost them." (Audience-relative: these are fine in research-journal register; the rule is calibrate has-the-reader-seen-X to the venue.)

## Research feature articles
7. Largely comprehensible to most AMS members while holding experts' interest; "start gently"; intro target audience ≈ "mathematicians who have never taken a graduate course in your specialty"; first pages intelligible to the general member.
8. Length discipline: 8-12 pages target, 15 absolute cap; ≤20 references (surveys may exceed only with editor permission). References "guide the reader to the literature" — curated, not exhaustive.

## Other types (calibration data points)
9. Community articles: scholarly tone; "inappropriate to use inflammatory language, sweeping generalizations unsupported by evidence, or personal attacks"; well-argued respectful opinions welcome.
10. "What is…?": 2-4 pages, ≤5 references; "aim for the shortest path" answering exactly: What is X? Why is X interesting?
11. Memorials: focus on the person, not the rememberers; no eulogy compilations ("I will miss X very much" banned); pictures; quotes must each serve a purpose.
12. History: based on current/recent research; avoid historians' technical language; 8-10pp, ≤20 refs.
13. Education: 5-10pp, ≤20 refs.

## Meta-point for the rubric
14. Every article type has an explicit LENGTH BUDGET and REFERENCE BUDGET — venue conformance is checkable arithmetic, not judgment.
