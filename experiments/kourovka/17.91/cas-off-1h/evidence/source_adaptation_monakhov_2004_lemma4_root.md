Monakhov's Lemma 4 supplies the affine primitive reduction for the maximal-subgroup problem. For C=core_G(M), the quotient G/C is primitive soluble with primitivator M/C, so it has an elementary abelian minimal normal subgroup V complemented by M/C. Since V is abelian, the extension inequality gives d(G/C)<=d(M/C)+1<=d(M)+1. Therefore G^(d(M)+1)<=C. This settles the core-free case with upper bound one and identifies the precise unresolved interface for the general problem: control the derived series of G after it enters C, using the fact that C is also a subgroup of M. The cited article itself does not provide such control, so this is partial rather than root-closing evidence.
