# Online adversarial result: not_refuted; a literature shortcut fails and a concrete one-group probe is identified

Target attacked: the interface debt_nonabelian_core_one_layer_rev0, namely whether a nonabelian core or Frattini lift can add a third unmatched derived layer.

The July 3, 2026 version of E. I. Khukhro and V. D. Mazurov (eds.), Unsolved Problems in Group Theory: The Kourovka Notebook, No. 21, p. 111, Problem 17.91, still prints the exact question without a star, answer, or solution comment (https://kourovkanotebookorg.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/07/21tkt.pdf). Its preface explains that solutions appearing after 2022 remain in the main section but receive comments. Thus the current authoritative problem list records no resolution. This is not a proof that no unpublished solution exists, but it rules out treating the Notebook or its update history as a cited solution of the root problem.

A sharper hypothesis mismatch occurs in Victor Monakhov and Alexander Trofimuk, “Invariants of finite solvable groups,” Algebra and Discrete Mathematics 14 (2012), no. 1, 107–131. On pp. 127–129, Theorem 30 gives n(M)=n(G)-i with i in {0,1,2} for nilpotent length, Theorem 31 gives the analogous F-length result for subgroup-closed saturated formations, and Question 9 then states the present derived-length problem verbatim (https://www.mathnet.ru/php/getFT.phtml?jrnid=adm&paperid=87&what=fullt). The saturation hypothesis cannot be silently transferred to derived length. For any r, an r-fold regular wreath product of nontrivial cyclic p-groups is a finite p-group of derived length r, whereas its Frattini quotient is elementary abelian. Hence the formation of groups of derived length at most k is not saturated: P/Phi(P) can have derived length 1 while P has derived length greater than k. The adjacent F-length theorem therefore supplies no upper bound for d(G)-d(M). The smallest unsupported hypothesis in that route is precisely saturation across Frattini/core extensions.

A concrete full-hypothesis stress test emerges from Raimundo Bastos and Deyfila Lima, “On maximal cyclic subgroups of finite groups,” Bulletin of the Australian Mathematical Society, published online 12 February 2026, DOI 10.1017/S0004972725100920. Table 1 records G=SmallGroup(1296,2890) with d(G)=6 (https://doi.org/10.1017/S0004972725100920). Since 1296=2^4*3^4, G is finite soluble. Enumerating its conjugacy classes of maximal subgroups tests genuine pairs (G,M), not an orbit-size shadow.

Two competing structural conjectures are now sharply separated. Counterexample conjecture C1: some maximal subgroup M of SmallGroup(1296,2890) has d(M) at most 3, producing d(G)-d(M) at least 3 and a validator-ready counterexample to the proposed bound k=2. Barrier conjecture C2: every maximal subgroup has derived length at least 4, so this smallest published derived-length-six host produces no gap-three pair; the search should then move specifically to its other order-1296 derived-length-six companions and to explicit one-layer Frattini extensions, while the positive branch must prove why every layer raising d(G) also raises d(M).

Verdict: not_refuted. No maximal subgroup of the cited group is asserted without computation, so this is not a candidate_counterexample artifact. It is an actionable obstruction to the formation-theoretic shortcut and a bounded decisive probe of the active root gap.
