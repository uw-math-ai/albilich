# Orthogonal semilinear decomposition-normalizer construction

## Local theorem
Fix distinct primes p and q. Put R=max(p,q), L=lcm(1,2,...,2R), E=4L, and n0=4RE. Let F be a finite field and let (V,Q) be a nondegenerate quadratic space of dimension n at least n0. In characteristic two, restrict here to even-dimensional D-type spaces; odd-dimensional B-type groups in characteristic two are the already integrated symplectic family. Put S=PΩ(V).

Let G be the subgroup of Aut(S) induced by projective semilinear similarities of (V,Q). Fix a hyperbolic E-space U in V, write V=U perpendicular W, let M be the decomposition stabilizer in S, and put D=N_G(M).

Then:

1. For each r in {p,q}, every element alpha of G of order dividing r is S-conjugate into D.
2. D is proper and maps onto G/S.
3. For every t at least 1, every order-r element of G wr Sym(t) is S^t-conjugate into D wr Sym(t).
4. Consequently no X with S^t contained in X contained in G wr Sym(t) is invariably generated by elements of orders p and q.

Thus the quadratic-space construction, discriminant/Witt-type control, spinor/Dickson correction, graph realization inside the semilinear group, and wreath-product gauge all close. The only missing theorem needed to replace G by Aut(S) is the exact high-rank abstract automorphism classification stated at the end.

## 1. Linear similarities: bounded nondegenerate blocks
Let alpha be represented by a linear similarity A with multiplier mu, and suppose alpha has order dividing the prime r. The identity case is immediate. Otherwise A^r=cI for some c in F*. Let b be the polar form of Q. Regard V as a module over

R_A=F[T,T^{-1}]/(T^r-c),

with T acting as A. This algebra has F-dimension r and is a quotient of a principal ideal ring, so every indecomposable R_A-module is cyclic primary and has dimension at most r.

The adjoint relation

b(Tv,w)=b(v,T* w),   T*=mu T^{-1}

makes R_A an algebra with involution. It is compatible with T^r=c because A^r=cI implies mu^r=c^2. The form b therefore identifies V with its involutive dual as an R_A-module.

Apply Krull-Schmidt. Modulo the categorical radical, the Gram morphism of b is a nonsingular Hermitian matrix over a product of division rings. If a diagonal entry is invertible, the corresponding indecomposable summand is b-nondegenerate. If no diagonal entry is invertible, nonsingularity gives an invertible off-diagonal entry; Hermitian symmetry then makes the corresponding two-by-two principal block invertible modulo the radical. Nilpotence of the radical lifts this to a b-nondegenerate sum of two indecomposables. Its orthogonal complement is A-invariant because A is a similarity. Iteration gives

V=V_1 perpendicular ... perpendicular V_m,

where every V_i is A-invariant, Q|V_i is nondegenerate, and dim(V_i) is at most 2r. This remains valid when char(F)=r and T^r-c is inseparable. In characteristic two, nondegeneracy of b on each block is precisely nondegeneracy of the even-dimensional quadratic restriction relevant to D-type groups.

## 2. Four-copy Witt cancellation fixes the type obstruction
A dimension-only pigeonhole is insufficient: blocks of equal dimension can have different discriminants or Arf invariants. For each e at most 2r, however, finite fields have at most two isometry types of nondegenerate e-dimensional quadratic spaces. Moreover, the Witt group of a finite field has exponent dividing four: in odd characteristic it is C2 x C2 or C4, and for nonsingular quadratic forms in characteristic two it has exponent two.

For each e dividing E, put N_e=E/e. The definition E=4L makes N_e divisible by four. If no dimension and isometry type occurred at least N_e times, then for each e the total number m_e of e-dimensional blocks would satisfy m_e less than 2E/e. Hence

n=sum_e e m_e < sum_{e=1}^{2r} 2E = 4rE at most 4RE,

contrary to n at least n0. Therefore N_e mutually isometric blocks exist for some e. Their orthogonal sum U' has dimension E and Witt class

[U']=N_e[V_i]=0.

Since E is even, U' is hyperbolic. Both U' and its orthogonal complement are A-invariant. This is the exact discriminant/Arf repair absent from a naive translation of the symplectic argument.

## 3. Nontrivial field component: descend only the needed hyperbolic summand
Now let alpha be represented by a semilinear similarity A sigma with nontrivial field component sigma. Since alpha has prime order r, sigma has order r. Put K=F^sigma. Projective order r gives

A sigma(A) ... sigma^{r-1}(A)=cI.

Cyclically permuting the product shows c belongs to K*. Norm surjectivity for finite fields gives d in F* with Norm_{F/K}(d)=c^{-1}. Replacing A by dA does not change the projective transformation and produces a semilinear map T=dA sigma satisfying T^r=1.

Galois descent gives a K-space V_0=Fix(T) of dimension n with V=F tensor_K V_0. Let nu be the semilinear multiplier, so

Q(Tv)=nu sigma(Q(v)).

The equality T^r=1 gives Norm_{F/K}(nu)=1. Scalar Hilbert 90 supplies a in F* with sigma(a)=a nu. For v in V_0,

sigma(aQ(v))=sigma(a)nu^{-1}Q(v)=aQ(v).

Thus Q_0(v)=aQ(v) is a K-valued nondegenerate quadratic form on V_0, and its scalar extension is aQ on V.

Every nondegenerate quadratic space over a finite field has anisotropic dimension at most two. Since n is much larger than E+2, Q_0 contains a hyperbolic E-dimensional subspace U_0. Its scalar extension U'=F tensor_K U_0 is T-invariant and hyperbolic for Q; its orthogonal complement is also T-invariant. This treats field, diagonal-field, graph-field, and characteristic-two field cases uniformly.

This local descent is deliberately weaker than conjugating alpha to a pure coordinate field automorphism. The stronger global conjugacy can fail as a proof method when an even-degree extension changes the discriminant of a descended even-dimensional form. Only the required hyperbolic summand descends without that obstruction.

## 4. Correction from O(V) to Ω(V)
In either case there is an alpha-invariant hyperbolic E-space U'. Witt extension gives x in O(V) with x(U)=U'. Hence alpha^x preserves U perpendicular W and lies in D.

It remains to replace x by an S-conjugator. The block isometry subgroup O(U) x O(W) maps onto O(V)/Ω(V). In odd characteristic this quotient is detected by determinant and spinor norm. A hyperbolic plane in U represents every nonzero scalar: Q(e+af)=a. Reflections in such vectors realize every pair consisting of determinant -1 and a prescribed spinor square class, and products of two reflections realize determinant +1 with either spinor class. In characteristic two, O(V)/Ω(V) is detected by the Dickson invariant, and swapping the two singular basis vectors of a hyperbolic plane in U realizes the nonzero Dickson class.

Choose d in the block isometry subgroup with xS=dS and put s=xd^{-1} in S. Since d normalizes the fixed decomposition,

alpha^s=d(alpha^x)d^{-1}

lies in D. This proves the required S-conjugacy and explicitly closes the spinor-norm and Dickson-invariant correction.

## 5. Outer-coset realization and graph cases
The same block subgroup realizes every projective similarity coset. On the hyperbolic space U, every multiplier lambda is realized by scaling one member of each hyperbolic pair by lambda and fixing the other. The complement W admits every multiplier when dim(W) is even: its hyperbolic part handles lambda and, in minus type, multiplication in the anisotropic norm plane realizes lambda because the finite-field norm is surjective. When dim(W) is odd, the possible similarity multipliers are precisely squares, and scalar multiplication realizes them. Combining this with the determinant-spinor or Dickson representatives above shows that the block stabilizer maps onto PGO(V)/PΩ(V).

Every field automorphism preserves the dimension and Witt type of Q. The hyperbolic form on U is defined over the prime field, while Q^sigma|W and Q|W have the same finite-field type; an isometry between those two restrictions gives a semilinear representative of the field coset preserving U perpendicular W. For D_m with m at least 5, the non-triality graph automorphism is induced by an improper isometry and is already among these PGO representatives. There is no B_m graph operation. Dimension n at least n0 excludes D4 triality. Thus D maps onto G/S.

Because n is greater than 2E, U and W have unequal dimensions. The normalizer of their block stabilizer must preserve U, whereas S contains root elements mixing U and W. Hence D is proper.

## 6. Wreath-product gauge
Let z=(g_1,...,g_t)pi in G wr Sym(t) have order dividing r. Every cycle of pi has length one or r. At a fixed point, the corresponding label has order dividing r and the local theorem moves it into D by an S-conjugation.

On an r-cycle, z^r=1 says that the ordered product of its labels is the identity in G. A G-valued coordinate gauge therefore trivializes all labels on that cycle. For each gauge entry u_i choose d_i in D with u_iS=d_iS, possible because D maps onto G/S, and put s_i=u_i d_i^{-1} in S. Replacing the original gauge by the S-valued gauge (s_i) leaves every resulting label in D. Treating cycles independently gives an S^t-conjugate of z in D wr Sym(t).

If S^t is contained in X contained in G wr Sym(t), independently conjugate proposed generators of orders p and q into D wr Sym(t). They then lie in the common proper subgroup X intersect (D wr Sym(t)), which cannot contain S^t. Hence they do not invariably generate X.

## Exact remaining theorem-level gap
The construction above applies to every automorphism induced by a projective semilinear quadratic similarity. To obtain the full orthogonal bridge, it remains to prove internally the following single classification interface:

(OA) For every sufficiently high-rank finite simple orthogonal group S=PΩ(V) of type B_m over odd characteristic or type D_m over arbitrary characteristic, with m at least 5, every abstract automorphism of S is induced by a projective semilinear similarity of Q.

A complete local proof must reconstruct the unlabelled B_m or D_m building from Sylow defining-characteristic normalizers and minimal parabolics; show that the only D_m diagram operation swaps the two terminal maximal-singular types and preserves point and line types; prove the orthogonal polar-space coordinate theorem in both odd and even characteristic, including anisotropic kernels of dimensions one and two; and prove that the chamber-action kernel is trivial. Odd-dimensional characteristic-two groups must be explicitly handed to the already verified symplectic theorem. No external citation is used here, so these coordinate steps remain a genuine debt rather than a hidden black box.

## Five-step proof spine
1. Twisted self-dual module decomposition gives nondegenerate invariant blocks of dimension at most 2r.
2. Four-copy Witt cancellation selects an invariant hyperbolic E-space with field-independent discriminant and Arf type.
3. Nontrivial semilinear components descend to a quadratic K-space and supply a hyperbolic E-space without requiring global form descent.
4. Determinant-spinor or Dickson correction, block similarity factors, graph representatives, properness, and the wreath gauge prove the theorem for the semilinear automorphism group G.
5. The sole remaining local gap is (OA), which upgrades G to Aut(S).

After (OA), the verified PSL, PSp, and PSU bridges together with this orthogonal bridge leave only the previously identified terminal bounded-rank, exceptional-family, and projective-degree CFSG assembly at the root level.

## Strongest near miss and self-check
The strongest failed route was the direct symplectic-style pigeonhole: equal-dimensional quadratic blocks need not have the same type, so their sum need not match one fixed decomposition. Passing from dimension to the Witt class and taking a multiplicity divisible by four removes exactly that obstruction.

The proof checks defining characteristic, inseparable module algebras, both even-dimensional quadratic signs, odd-dimensional spaces in odd characteristic, characteristic-two Arf type, determinant and spinor norm, the characteristic-two Dickson invariant, field and graph-field components, and exact identity label products in the wreath gauge. The numerical bound ensures the existence of the chosen hyperbolic subspaces and excludes triality. The only unsupported statement is explicitly isolated as (OA); no claim about the terminal CFSG assembly is made.
