# Proof dossier: the high-dimensional PSL outer-coset normalizer theorem

## Theorem

Fix distinct primes p and q. Put R=max{p,q}, L=lcm(1,2,...,R), and n0=RL. Then n0>2L. Let F be any finite field, let n>=n0, let V=F^n, and let S=PSL(V). Fix a decomposition V=U direct_sum W with dim U=L, let M be the image of GL(U) x GL(W) in PGL(V), and put D_L=N_Aut(S)(M).

For every r in {p,q} and every alpha in Aut(S) of order dividing r, there is s in S such that alpha^s lies in D_L. Moreover D_L maps onto Aut(S)/S. Consequently, for every t>=1, every element of order dividing r in Aut(S) wr Sym(t) is S^t-conjugate into D_L wr Sym(t). The subgroup D_L is proper.

Thus the decisive PSL outer-coset obligation holds uniformly in F and n, including inner-diagonal, diagonal-field, graph, and graph-field cosets.

## 1. Inner-diagonal elements

Let alpha be represented by A in GL_n(F), with [A] in PGL_n(F) of order dividing the prime r. Then A^r=lambda I for some lambda in F*. If char(F) is not r, the polynomial T^r-lambda is separable, so V is a direct sum of irreducible A-modules of dimensions at most r. If char(F)=r, finite fields are perfect, so lambda=mu^r and (A-mu I)^r=0; the Jordan blocks again have dimensions at most r. In either case V is a direct sum of A-invariant indecomposable blocks whose dimensions lie in {1,...,r}.

Let m_e be the number of e-dimensional blocks. Since every e<=r divides L, if m_e<L/e for every e, then

n=sum_e e m_e <= sum_{e=1}^r e(L/e-1)=rL-r(r+1)/2<rL,

contrary to n>=RL>=rL. Hence some e occurs at least L/e times. The sum of L/e such blocks is an A-invariant L-space and the sum of all remaining blocks is an invariant complement. A projective change of basis therefore puts alpha in the normalizer of an L+(n-L) decomposition.

## 2. Cyclic matrix descent

We use the following elementary matrix form of Hilbert 90. Let F/K be a finite cyclic extension generated by sigma of order m. If B in GL_n(F) satisfies

B sigma(B) ... sigma^{m-1}(B)=I,

then B=X sigma(X)^{-1} for some X in GL_n(F).

Indeed, T(v)=B sigma(v) is a sigma-semilinear map with T^m=1. Cyclic Galois descent gives an F-basis fixed by T. For completeness, the descent proof uses the trace sums sum_i T^i(cv): Artin independence of the distinct maps sigma^i ensures that every nonzero T-stable quotient has a nonzero fixed vector. Successively lifting such vectors is possible because the only lifting obstruction is an additive cyclic cocycle, and ker(Tr_{F/K})=(sigma-1)F. Induction on the dimension produces n F-linearly independent fixed vectors. Taking them as the columns of X gives B sigma(X)=X, as required.

## 3. Diagonal-field elements

Suppose the field projection of alpha has nontrivial order r. Write alpha=[A]sigma, where sigma has order r and K=F^sigma. The equation alpha^r=1 says

A sigma(A) ... sigma^{r-1}(A)=lambda I.

Cyclically shifting the product shows sigma(lambda)=lambda, so lambda lies in K*. The norm F* to K* is surjective. Choose c in F* with Norm(c)=lambda^{-1}. Then B=cA is an exact matrix cocycle, and Section 2 supplies X with B=X sigma(X)^{-1}. Conjugation by [X] therefore sends alpha to the pure field automorphism sigma. The coordinate field automorphism preserves the fixed decomposition U direct_sum W, so it lies in D_L.

This proves the field and diagonal-field cases before the determinant correction below. Notice that the bound on L is not needed in this case.

## 4. Graph and graph-field involutions

A graph component can occur only when r=2. Since p and q are distinct, R>=3 and hence L is even. Write tau for inverse transpose and write

alpha=[A] tau sigma,

where sigma is either the identity or a field automorphism of order two. From alpha^2=1 we obtain

A sigma(A)^{-T}=lambda I,

or equivalently A=lambda sigma(A)^T.

If sigma is nontrivial, then lambda sigma(lambda)=1. Multiplicative Hilbert 90 in F* gives c with lambda=sigma(c)/c. Replacing A by the projectively equivalent matrix cA makes A=sigma(A)^T. Thus A is the Gram matrix of a nondegenerate sigma-Hermitian form.

If sigma is trivial, then lambda^2=1. In odd characteristic, A is either symmetric or skew-symmetric; in the latter case it is alternating. In characteristic two it is symmetric, possibly alternating.

Each resulting form has a nondegenerate L-dimensional subspace. Hermitian forms and symmetric forms in odd characteristic admit orthogonal bases. Alternating forms admit symplectic bases, and L is even. For a nonalternating symmetric form in characteristic two, repeatedly split off nonsingular one-spaces until the remaining summand is alternating. If the alternating remainder has dimension at least L, take an L-dimensional symplectic summand. Otherwise take the whole alternating remainder together with enough of the mutually orthogonal nonsingular lines; both required dimensions are even, and n>2L guarantees that enough lines remain. In every case the orthogonal complement of the selected L-space is nondegenerate.

Choose a basis adapted to this orthogonal decomposition. Under the corresponding congruence, the matrix A becomes block diagonal of sizes L and n-L. The automorphism [A]tau sigma then normalizes M and hence belongs to D_L after a projective conjugation.

## 5. Correction from PGL-conjugacy to PSL-conjugacy

The determinant class identifies

PGL_n(F)/PSL_n(F) with F*/(F*)^n.

The block-diagonal subgroup M maps surjectively onto this quotient: diag(d,1,...,1), with its first coordinate in U, realizes the class of any d in F*. Suppose a projective element x found in Sections 1, 3, or 4 satisfies alpha^x in D_L. Choose k in M with the same determinant class as x. Then s=xk^{-1} lies in S and

alpha^s=(alpha^x)^{k^{-1}} lies in D_L.

This proves the required conjugacy by the socle, not merely by PGL.

The same observation also proves that D_L maps onto every outer coset. Its block-diagonal elements realize all diagonal classes, coordinate Frobenius maps realize all field classes, and tau realizes the graph class. These elements generate Aut(S)/S in the standard automorphism description of PSL_n(F).

## 6. Wreath-product gauge

Let z=(g_1,...,g_t)pi in Aut(S) wr Sym(t) have order dividing the prime r. Every cycle of pi has length 1 or r.

On a fixed point of pi, the corresponding label g_i has order dividing r, so the local theorem gives an S-conjugate in D_L.

On an r-cycle, z^r=1 says that the ordered product of its edge labels is the identity. Hence an Aut(S)-valued coordinate gauge can recursively trivialize all labels on that cycle. Let its gauge entries be u_i. Since D_L maps onto Aut(S)/S, choose d_i in D_L with u_iS=d_iS and put s_i=u_i d_i^{-1} in S. Replacing the u_i-gauge by the s_i-gauge changes the trivial labels only to products of elements d_i and d_j^{-1}, all lying in D_L. Treating the factor cycles independently gives an element of S^t conjugating z into D_L wr Sym(t).

## 7. Properness and chief-factor consequence

The subgroup D_L is proper because S does not normalize the fixed proper decomposition: an elementary transvection mixing U and W lies outside its stabilizer. Equivalently, M is not normal in S.

Therefore, if

S^t <= X <= Aut(S) wr Sym(t)

and a,b in X have orders p and q, independent S^t-conjugates of a and b lie in D_L wr Sym(t). They consequently generate a subgroup of the proper subgroup X intersect (D_L wr Sym(t)); properness follows because X contains S^t whereas D_L wr Sym(t) does not. Thus no such X is invariably generated by elements of orders p and q.

Combined with the existing alternating-chief-factor argument, this excludes alternating factors of degree greater than pq and all PSL factors of natural dimension at least RL from any prospective host invariably generated by the prescribed orders. The remaining root-level work is the analogous uniform theorem for the symplectic, unitary, and orthogonal families, followed by the bounded-family and projective-representation-degree assembly.

## Self-check

The bound n>=RL is independent of F and exceeds 2L because R>=3. The inner-diagonal proof selects whole rational or Jordan blocks, so it supplies an invariant complement rather than only an invariant subspace. The field proof removes the scalar cocycle using norm surjectivity before applying matrix descent. The graph proof covers diagonal-graph and diagonal-graph-field coefficients through the full congruence equation. The determinant correction stays inside the fixed decomposition normalizer. On nontrivial wreath cycles the product is the identity in Aut(S), not merely modulo S, because z^r=1. No conclusion about the unresolved non-PSL classical families is claimed.
