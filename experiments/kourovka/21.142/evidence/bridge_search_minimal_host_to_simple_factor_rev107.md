# Bidirectional bridge search: arbitrary hosts to simple factors

The forward frontier consists of the integrated high-dimensional PSL, PSp, PSU, and orthogonal common-overgroup theorems, all formulated for groups between S^t and Aut(S) wr Sym(t). The backward root obligation begins with an arbitrary finite host containing A_m. The exact missing interface is therefore not another orthogonal automorphism theorem: it is the minimal-host lemma which puts an arbitrary host into the required wreath interval and proves that A_m embeds in the simple factor S rather than surviving only through factor permutations.

The selected bridge is proved in the companion dossier. Its only remaining root obligation is the terminal analysis of simple factors containing A_m outside the already integrated high-dimensional classical families.
