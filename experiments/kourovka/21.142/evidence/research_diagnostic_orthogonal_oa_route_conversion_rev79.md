## Exact missing interface

The single unresolved theorem-level gap on this spine segment is the source-certified OA equality.

## Two proof attacks checked

The intrinsic-building attack represents singular-subspace stabilizers as parabolics and their intersections as flag incidence. It becomes a proof only after establishing that these parabolics are intrinsically recognizable and that the resulting chamber action is faithful; neither assertion is proved in the candidate artifact. The direct automorphism-classification attack would instead invoke an exact theorem identifying Aut(PΩ(V)) with the semilinear-similarity image, after checking all family, rank, field, sign, kernel, and exceptional-case hypotheses. This is the shorter serious route, but no locatable theorem statement or proof is present in the available offline evidence.

The subgroup-quotient shortcut is circular: listing inner-diagonal, field, and graph automorphisms inside the semilinear group does not prove that no additional abstract automorphisms exist.

## Conditional local deduction

Assume the exact OA equality. Let alpha be any abstract automorphism of S whose order divides p or q. By OA, alpha is induced by a projective semilinear quadratic similarity. The verified orthogonal inference then supplies an S-conjugate of alpha in the fixed proper nondegenerate-decomposition normalizer and supplies all outer cosets needed for the factor-cycle gauge. Applying the same argument independently on every factor of S^t gives the common proper product-type subgroup required for the orthogonal family. Thus OA is exactly the missing interface; no further orthogonal construction is needed.
