Target attacked: debt_orthogonal_abstract_automorphism_interface_rev69 (OA), asserting that every abstract automorphism of a sufficiently high-rank finite simple orthogonal group is induced by a projective semilinear quadratic similarity.

Result: not_refuted. The proposed counterexample mechanism—an exotic abstract automorphism outside the semilinear-similarity group—is blocked by published classification theorems throughout the target range.

Competing structural conjectures tested:
(1) Exotic-automorphism conjecture: for infinitely many B_m(q) or D_m^±(q), m≥5, Aut(PΩ(V)) contains an automorphism not induced by a semilinear similarity of Q. Such a family would kill OA and the orthogonal common-overgroup route.
(2) Standard-automorphism conjecture: outside D_4 triality, every automorphism is a product of inner, diagonal, field, and ordinary diagram automorphisms, and each factor is induced by a projective semilinear similarity. This would eliminate the exotic-automorphism attack and reduce OA to citation adaptation.

Decisive source 1: Robert Steinberg, “Automorphisms of Finite Linear Groups,” Canadian Journal of Mathematics 12 (1960), 606–615, DOI 10.4153/CJM-1960-054-6. Section 2, pp. 607–608, includes normal types B_l and D_l and twisted type ^2D_l, identifying them with the corresponding projective orthogonal groups. Theorem 3.2 on p. 608 states, in complete local form, that every automorphism of any finite group in that list has a decomposition α=g f d i, with i inner, d diagonal, f field, and g graph; the proof is completed on pp. 612–613. Section 3 distinguishes the exceptional S_3 graph group of D_4 from the single ordinary D_l diagram involution in higher rank. Source: https://doi.org/10.4153/CJM-1960-054-6.

Decisive source 2: Edward A. Connors, “Automorphism of Orthogonal Groups in Characteristic 2,” Journal of Number Theory 5 (1973), 477–501, DOI 10.1016/0022-314X(73)90017-6. Its main theorem applies to a nondefective quadratic space over a characteristic-two field with dimension at least 10 and more than two field elements, and to Δ=Ω(V): every abstract automorphism of Δ is conjugation by a semilinear bijection g satisfying Q(gx)=a·Q(x)^u for a nonzero scalar a and a field automorphism u. Thus for D_m(q), m≥5 and q≥4 even, it directly gives precisely the semilinear-similarity conclusion. Source: https://doi.org/10.1016/0022-314X(73)90017-6.

Hypothesis check and local deduction: B_m(q) with q odd and D_m^±(q) in odd characteristic are among Steinberg’s normal or twisted orthogonal types. D_m^±(q) in characteristic two has dimension 2m≥10; Connors covers q>2 directly, while Steinberg also covers q=2. In characteristic two Ω(V)=PΩ(V) because the relevant scalar center is trivial. The manifest-listed artifact proof_orthogonal_quadratic_witt_descent_rev69 already supplies the remaining translation: inner and diagonal factors are induced by projective orthogonal similarities, field factors by semilinear similarities, and for D_m with m≥5 the unique nontriality diagram involution is induced by an improper isometry; B_m has no diagram automorphism. D_4 triality is expressly outside the asserted range, and odd-dimensional characteristic-two B-type is assigned to the symplectic branch.

Representation switch: in the subgroup/quotient representation, the question is whether Out(S) has a component beyond inner-diagonal, field, and graph factors; Steinberg says no. In the coordinate representation, those factors become conjugations by semilinear maps preserving Q up to a scalar and field twist; Connors establishes this directly in even characteristic. In the building representation, the only higher-rank D_m type permutation swaps the terminal nodes and is realized by the improper isometry already present locally. The round trip therefore leaves no candidate exotic component.

Outcome-dependent decision: finding an extra automorphism component would have required abandoning the orthogonal route and sending a concrete family to counterexample validation. The actual outcome instead blocks that refutation path.
