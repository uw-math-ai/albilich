# Automorphisms of high-rank finite symplectic groups

## Local theorem
Let F be a finite field of characteristic ell, let V be a 2m-dimensional nondegenerate symplectic F-space, and suppose m>=3. Put S=PSp(V). Every abstract automorphism of S is induced by a semilinear symplectic similitude. Equivalently, after fixing coordinates, every automorphism has the form [A] sigma with A in GSp(V) and sigma a field automorphism of F. Thus Aut(S) is generated by inner-diagonal and field automorphisms; there is no graph component in rank m>=3.

## Proof
We reconstruct the symplectic building from the abstract group, so the argument applies to every abstract automorphism and does not assume the desired classification.

Choose a symplectic basis and a complete totally isotropic flag. Let U be the corresponding upper-unitriangular symplectic subgroup and let B be the flag stabilizer. Direct symplectic Gaussian elimination gives the split BN-pair of S: U is a Sylow ell-subgroup, B=N_S(U), and the minimal proper overgroups P_1,...,P_m of B are the stabilizers obtained by deleting one member of the flag. For clarity, the overgroup assertion follows from the same elimination. Bruhat reduction writes each element as b_1 w b_2 with b_i in B and w in the Weyl group W(C_m). If H contains B, shortening a reduced word for every Weyl element represented in H shows that the represented Weyl elements form the standard parabolic subgroup generated by those simple reflections s_i for which P_i is contained in H. Hence H is the corresponding standard parabolic. In particular, the minimal overgroups of B are exactly the P_i.

Let phi be an automorphism of S. It sends U to a Sylow ell-subgroup, so after composing phi with an inner automorphism we may assume phi(U)=U. Then phi(B)=B and phi permutes P_1,...,P_m. The incidence geometry whose chambers are cosets of B and whose i-adjacency is given by cosets of P_i is therefore preserved by phi, possibly with a permutation of types. The Coxeter number m_ij is recovered as half the girth of an {i,j}-residue, so the type permutation preserves the labelled Coxeter diagram. For C_m with m>=3 this diagram has no nontrivial automorphism: it is a path with a unique edge labelled 4 at one end. Consequently phi is type-preserving on the building.

Identify this building with the flag geometry of nonzero totally isotropic subspaces of V. On type-one vertices phi induces a bijection f of the projective points of V. Two distinct points L and M are orthogonal exactly when they lie in a common type-two vertex, so f preserves symplectic orthogonality in both directions. It consequently maps every projective line to a projective line. Indeed, an isotropic line is already a type-two vertex. If L and M are not orthogonal, their projective line is recovered solely from the orthogonality relation as the point set of

((L-perp intersect M-perp)-perp)=span(L,M).

Thus the fundamental projective-coordinate argument applies: choose a projective frame; preservation of its coordinate lines gives one common additive and multiplicative bijection of F, hence a field automorphism sigma, and a sigma-semilinear bijection Z of V inducing f.

The map Z is a semilinear symplectic similitude. To see this without invoking another classification theorem, define the F-bilinear alternating form

C(v,w)=sigma^{-1}(B(Zv,Zw)).

The forms C and B have identical orthogonality relations. For every nonzero v, the linear forms C(v,-) and B(v,-) have the same kernel and hence are scalar multiples. Applying this to v, w, and v+w for independent v,w shows that the scalar is independent of v. Therefore C=dB for one d in F*, or

B(Zv,Zw)=sigma(d) sigma(B(v,w)).

Hence Z is a sigma-semilinear similitude and conjugation by Z induces an automorphism theta_Z of S with the same building action as phi.

It remains to show that no automorphism is hidden in the kernel of the building action. Put delta=theta_Z^{-1}phi. Since delta fixes every chamber, delta(x)B=xB for every x in S, so delta(x)=x b_x with b_x in B. For arbitrary y, applying delta to xy and using that both xyB and yB are fixed gives b_x yB=yB. Thus y^{-1}b_x y lies in B for every y, and b_x belongs to the core of B in S. The group S is simple and B is proper, so this core is trivial. Hence delta is the identity and phi=theta_Z.

Writing Z=A sigma in the fixed coordinates gives A in GSp(V), proving that phi is an inner-diagonal similitude followed by a field automorphism.

## Exception check for the target range
For distinct primes p and q, R=max{p,q}>=3 and E=lcm(1,...,2R)>=60. The target hypothesis n=2m>=2RE therefore gives n>=360 and m>=180. It is far outside rank one, the nonsimple PSp_4(2) case, and all type-C_2 graph or exceptional-isogeny phenomena. The proof above actually covers every field, including F_2, once m>=3. Any exceptional isomorphism in this range cannot create an additional automorphism, because the reconstruction began with an arbitrary abstract automorphism of S and forced it to act as a semilinear similitude on the intrinsic building.

## Five-step proof spine
1. A Sylow defining-characteristic subgroup and its normalizer intrinsically recover the C_m chamber system.
2. Since the C_m Coxeter diagram has no symmetry for m>=3, every group automorphism acts type-preservingly.
3. Orthogonality reconstructs all projective lines, forcing the building action to come from a semilinear symplectic similitude.
4. The kernel of the building action is the core of a Borel subgroup and is trivial.
5. This exhaustive automorphism description plugs into the two existing local cases and completes the selected route.

## Self-check
The proof starts with an arbitrary abstract automorphism; it does not assume preservation of transvections. It handles q=2 because the Sylow-normalizer and Bruhat arguments do not require a nontrivial split torus. Rank m>=3 is used both for the polar building and for rigidity of the labelled C_m diagram. The projective collineation is shown to preserve the form up to a scalar and the same field automorphism. Passage to PSp is legitimate because S is centerless and simple in this range. The strongest failed approach was direct recognition of long-root transvection subgroups, which creates a difficult unnecessary centralizer calculation in characteristic two; the intrinsic parabolic-building reconstruction removes exactly that obstruction.
