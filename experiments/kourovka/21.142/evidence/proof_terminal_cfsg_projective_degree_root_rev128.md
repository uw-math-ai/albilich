# Terminal CFSG/projective-degree bridge

## Theorem
Fix distinct primes p and q. There is an integer m such that A_m does not embed in any finite group invariably generated by an element of order p and an element of order q. Consequently, the answer to Kourovka Problem 21.142 is negative.

## External theorem 1: uniform projective-degree bound for alternating groups
For a prime ell and a finite simple group T, let R_ell(T) be the least d for which T embeds in PGL_d(K) over a splitting field K of characteristic ell. Parker, Pientka, Seidel, and Stroth, *Finite groups which are almost groups of Lie type in characteristic p*, Memoirs of the AMS 292 (2023), no. 1452, Lemma C.4, state: if n >= 9, then for every prime ell,

R_ell(A_n) = n - 1 - delta_{n,ell} >= n - 2.

Paper_id: DOI 10.1090/memo/1452. Theorem_id: Lemma C.4. ArXiv id: none used. Their proof cites A. Wagner, *The faithful linear representations of least degree of S_n and A_n over a field of characteristic 2*, Math. Z. 151 (1976), 127-137; the odd-characteristic companion, Math. Z. 154 (1977), 103-114; and *An observation on the degrees of projective representations of the symmetric and alternating group over an arbitrary field*, Arch. Math. 29 (1977), 583-589.

Applicability: an embedding A_m -> PGL_d(F) over a finite field of characteristic ell remains injective after scalar extension to a splitting field, so d >= R_ell(A_m) >= m-2.

## External theorem 2: bounded defining-characteristic degree for exceptional groups
Bradford and Thom, *Short laws for finite groups of Lie type*, J. Eur. Math. Soc. 28 (2026), 4063-4103, Theorem A.2 and Table 2, define n(X;ell) as the minimum d such that the finite simple group X(q), in defining characteristic ell, embeds in PGL_d over the algebraic closure. They give

n(E6)=n(2E6)=27, n(E7)=56, n(E8)=248,
n(F4)=25 or 26, n(G2)=6 or 7,
n(3D4)=8, n(2B2)=4, n(2F4)=26, n(2G2)=7.

Thus every finite simple exceptional group of Lie type has a faithful projective representation in its defining characteristic of degree at most 248. Paper_id: DOI 10.4171/JEMS/1547. Theorem_id: Theorem A.2, Table 2. ArXiv id: none used. The theorem reproduces the defining-characteristic projective-degree conclusions of Kleidman-Liebeck, Proposition 5.4.13.

## External theorem 3: CFSG interface
The classification of finite simple groups says that every nonabelian finite simple group is alternating, classical of Lie type, exceptional of Lie type, or one of the 26 sporadic groups. This four-family formulation is recorded in Kleidman and Liebeck, *The Subgroup Structure of the Finite Classical Groups*, Cambridge University Press, 1990, Preface. Paper_id: DOI 10.1017/CBO9780511629235.001. Theorem_id: classification statement in the Preface. ArXiv id: none.

## Choice of the counterexample
For the fixed p and q, let b=b(p,q) be the maximum of the natural-dimension thresholds in the already verified PSL, PSp, PSU, and orthogonal common-overgroup claims. Choose m so that

1. m >= 9;
2. m-2 > b;
3. m-2 > 248;
4. m-2 >= pq+1; and
5. |A_m| exceeds the order of every sporadic simple group.

Such an m exists because there are only 26 sporadic groups.

## Terminal simple-factor lemma
Let S be a nonabelian finite simple group containing A_m.

If S=A_n is alternating, compose A_m -> A_n with the faithful permutation-matrix embedding A_n -> PGL_n(F_ell). Distinct permutation matrices have distinct projective classes, so the composite is faithful. Wagner's bound gives n >= m-2 >= pq+1. Therefore the verified alternating intransitive-wreath obstruction applies to every interval S^t <= X <= Aut(S) wr Sym(t).

If S is classical of natural dimension d, its defining projective natural representation gives S <= PGL_d(F) (with the usual projectivization for linear, unitary, symplectic, and orthogonal groups). Composing with A_m <= S and applying Wagner gives d >= m-2 > b. Hence S is in the high-dimensional range of the corresponding verified PSL, PSp, PSU, or orthogonal common-overgroup theorem. Characteristic-two odd orthogonal type is covered by the symplectic identification, as in the verified family dossiers.

If S is exceptional of Lie type, Bradford-Thom Theorem A.2 supplies S <= PGL_d(K) with d <= 248. Composition with A_m <= S would give m-2 <= d <= 248, contradicting the choice of m.

If S is sporadic, then |A_m| <= |S| would be necessary, contradicting condition 5. These cases exhaust S by CFSG. Thus every simple S containing A_m lies in an already verified alternating or high-dimensional classical common-overgroup family.

## Assembly to the root
Suppose A_m embedded in a finite group G invariably generated by x and y of respective orders p and q. Choose such a host of minimum order. The integrated minimal-host lemma gives a unique nonabelian socle S^t, an embedding

S^t <= G <= Aut(S) wr Sym(t),

and an injective coordinate projection A_m -> S. The terminal lemma places S in one of the verified common-overgroup families. Consequently x and y have independent S^t-conjugates in one common proper subgroup of G, contradicting invariable generation. Therefore no such host G exists, and A_m is a counterexample.

## Self-check
The argument uses the original fixed primes only through the verified family thresholds and pq+1. Wagner's result covers every finite characteristic, including 2 and characteristics dividing m. Scalar extension preserves injectivity. The socle factor is nonabelian, so cyclic simple groups do not occur. Alternating factors have degree at least m-2, which is enough for the existing n >= pq+1 obstruction; the stronger minimal-permutation-degree theorem is unnecessary. Exceptional field sizes may be unbounded, but their defining-characteristic projective degree is uniformly at most 248. Sporadic groups are removed by finiteness. No verification status is asserted here.
