# Proof dossier: high-dimensional symplectic outer-coset normalizer theorem

## Theorem

Fix distinct primes p and q. Put R=max{p,q}, E=lcm(1,2,...,2R), and n0=2RE. Let F be a finite field, let n=2m>=n0, let V=F^n carry its standard nondegenerate alternating form, and put S=PSp(V). Fix a nondegenerate E-space U with V=U orthogonal_sum W. Let M be the image in PGSp(V) of the pairs of similitudes of U and W having the same multiplier, and put D_E=N_Aut(S)(M).

For every r in {p,q} and every alpha in Aut(S) of order dividing r, some S-conjugate of alpha lies in D_E. The subgroup D_E is proper and maps onto Aut(S)/S. Consequently, for every t>=1, every element of order dividing r in Aut(S) wr Sym(t) is S^t-conjugate into D_E wr Sym(t). Hence no X with S^t<=X<=Aut(S) wr Sym(t) is invariably generated by elements of orders p and q.

## Lemma 1: bounded orthogonal blocks

Let A be a similitude of a finite-dimensional symplectic F-space and suppose A^r=lambda I. Then the space is an orthogonal direct sum of nondegenerate A-invariant subspaces of dimensions at most 2r.

Proof. The minimal polynomial of A divides T^r-lambda. Hence every indecomposable summand of the F[T]-module V has dimension at most r. The adjoint relation <Av,Aw>=mu<v,w>, where mu is the multiplier of A, equips F[A] with the involution A maps to mu A^{-1}; the alternating form identifies the module with its twisted dual. Pair each indecomposable isomorphism type with its twisted-dual type. A non-self-dual summand and its dual form a nondegenerate hyperbolic block of dimension at most 2r. For a self-dual type C, work over the local endomorphism ring End_{F[T]}(C). If the Gram matrix on the multiplicity space has a unit diagonal entry, one copy of C splits off nondegenerately. Otherwise nondegeneracy supplies a unit off-diagonal entry, and two copies split off as a nondegenerate block. This is the usual Gram elimination over a local ring; iterating it gives blocks containing one or two indecomposable module summands, hence of dimension at most 2r. Orthogonal complements remain A-invariant because A is a similitude. This proves the lemma in both semisimple and defining-characteristic cases.

## Inner-diagonal case

Represent alpha by A in GSp(V). Since alpha^r=1 projectively, A^r is scalar. Apply Lemma 1 and let m_e be the number of resulting blocks of dimension e. Every occurring e is at most 2r and divides E. If m_e<E/e for every e, then

n=sum_e e m_e <= sum_{e=1}^{2r} e(E/e-1)<2rE<=2RE,

contrary to n>=n0. Thus E/e blocks of one dimension e have a nondegenerate orthogonal sum U' of dimension E. Its orthogonal complement is A-invariant. A symplectic isometry carrying U' orthogonal_sum U'^perp to U orthogonal_sum W belongs to Sp(V), so an S-conjugate of alpha lies in M and hence in D_E.

## Field and diagonal-field case

Because m>=3, the type C_m diagram has no graph automorphism; the automorphisms of S are the inner-diagonal similitudes together with field automorphisms. Suppose the field projection of alpha is nontrivial. It then has order r. Write alpha=[A]sigma, where sigma generates Gal(F/K) of order r and A is a similitude. Projective order r gives

A sigma(A) ... sigma^{r-1}(A)=lambda I

with lambda in K*. Choose c in F* with Norm_{F/K}(c)=lambda^{-1}, and put B=cA. Then B sigma(B)...sigma^{r-1}(B)=I. Matrix Hilbert 90 gives X in GL(V) with B=X sigma(X)^{-1}. It remains to ensure that the conjugator is a similitude.

Let J be the standard alternating Gram matrix and let nu be the multiplier of B. Put J_X=X^T J X. The similitude equation for B yields J_X=nu sigma(J_X), and the cocycle equation gives Norm(nu)=1. Scalar Hilbert 90 supplies a in F* with sigma(a)=nu a. Hence aJ_X is defined over K. All nondegenerate alternating forms of dimension n over K are isometric, so there is Y in GL_n(K) with Y^T(aJ_X)Y=J. For Z=XY we have B=Z sigma(Z)^{-1} and Z^T J Z=a^{-1}J; thus Z lies in GSp(V). Projective conjugation by Z sends alpha to the coordinate field automorphism sigma, which preserves U orthogonal_sum W.

To replace this PGSp-conjugator by an S-conjugator, observe that M maps onto PGSp(V)/PSp(V): for every desired multiplier class, choose similitudes of U and W with that common multiplier and take their orthogonal sum. Choose d in M with dS=[Z]S and put s=[Z]d^{-1} in S. Since both sigma and d lie in D_E, alpha^s lies in D_E.

The same description proves that D_E maps onto Aut(S)/S: M supplies every inner-diagonal coset and the coordinate Frobenius automorphisms supply every field coset.

## Wreath-product gauge

Let z=(g_1,...,g_t)pi have order dividing r. Every cycle of pi has length 1 or r. At a fixed point, the corresponding g_i has order dividing r and the local theorem moves it into D_E by an S-conjugation. On an r-cycle, z^r=1 says that the ordered product of its edge labels is the identity. An Aut(S)-valued coordinate gauge therefore trivializes those labels. For each gauge entry u_i choose d_i in D_E representing u_iS and replace u_i by s_i=u_i d_i^{-1} in S. Relative to the trivial gauge, the new labels are products of elements d_i and d_j^{-1}, hence lie in D_E. Treating cycles independently gives the required S^t-conjugate in D_E wr Sym(t).

## Properness and chief-factor consequence

Since n>2E, the two summands have unequal dimensions. Thus D_E stabilizes the fixed proper nondegenerate E-space and is proper; equivalently, S contains symplectic transvections which do not normalize M. If S^t<=X<=Aut(S) wr Sym(t) and a,b in X have orders p,q, choose independent S^t-conjugates of a,b in D_E wr Sym(t). They generate a subgroup of X intersect (D_E wr Sym(t)), which is proper because it does not contain S^t. Hence X is not invariably generated by a,b.

## Root-local proof spine

1. Any finite host of a sufficiently large alternating group A_m has a nonabelian simple chief constituent containing A_m.
2. The existing alternating-chief-factor theorem excludes large alternating constituents.
3. The verified PSL outer-normalizer theorem excludes high-dimensional PSL constituents.
4. The theorem proved here excludes high-dimensional PSp constituents.
5. Exactly one theorem-level gap remains on this negative spine: exclude the remaining high-dimensional unitary and orthogonal constituents and perform the bounded-rank/representation-degree assembly. That residual theorem, together with Steps 1-4, yields an A_m counterexample for all sufficiently large m.

## Self-check and near miss

The bound is uniform in F, E is even, and n0>2E. The module argument treats nonsemisimple order-r transformations in characteristic r. Rank m>=3 excludes the only symplectic graph case, which occurs in bounded rank C_2. Plain GL Hilbert 90 was insufficient because its conjugator need not normalize the fixed symplectic group; descending the transported alternating form repairs exactly that failure. The quotient correction occurs inside M, so it preserves the chosen decomposition. The wreath argument uses identity label product in Aut(S), not merely modulo S. No claim about unitary or orthogonal groups is made.
