Shortest local proof spine for the first repair segment:

1. Chief-factor reduction: if A_m embeds in a finite host H, then A_m embeds in a nonabelian simple constituent S of a chief factor S^t of H.
2. Alternating obstruction: for n>pq, an A_n^t chief factor is incompatible with invariable generation by orders p and q.
3. Inner-diagonal PSL obstruction: in sufficiently large natural dimension, bounded-order projective transformations preserve a common bounded-dimensional direct summand, and the wreath-product gauge can be chosen in PSL_n(F)^t.
4. Outer-PSL bridge, presently unproved: field, graph, and graph-field automorphisms of orders p or q must, after PSL-conjugacy, lie in one proper two-block decomposition normalizer whose image contains the needed outer cosets.
5. Conditional assembly: Step 4 gives the corresponding Aut(PSL_n(F)) wr S_t gauge statement and completes the high-dimensional PSL chief-factor obstruction.

Dependency: Steps 2 and 3 motivate Step 4; Steps 3+4 imply Step 5. The single immediate theorem-level gap is Step 4. Even its proof would repair only the PSL segment; the advisor artifact itself supplies neither that proof nor the later all-classical/CFSG assembly needed for a root counterexample.

Representation check. In the maximal-overgroup representation, the missing assertion is that two prescribed prime-order classes have independent socle-conjugates in a common proper subgroup. In the semilinear-module representation, it becomes the concrete assertion that the corresponding semilinear or contragredient maps preserve, after PSL-conjugacy, a fixed-dimensional direct-sum decomposition and that its normalizer realizes their outer cosets. If the latter holds, coordinate-cycle labels in a wreath product can be trivialized and their gauges corrected into the socle using representatives from that normalizer. Conversely, the wreath assertion at t=1 gives the local common-normalizer assertion. Properness follows because the full socle does not preserve the chosen decomposition.
