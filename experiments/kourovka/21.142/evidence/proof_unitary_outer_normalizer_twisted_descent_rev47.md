# High-dimensional unitary outer-coset normalizer theorem

## Theorem
Fix distinct primes p and q. Put R=max(p,q), E=lcm(1,2,...,2R), and n0=2RE. Let k=F_(Q^2), with involution bar(x)=x^Q, and let V be an n-dimensional nondegenerate Hermitian k-space with n>=n0. Put S=PSU(V). Fix a nondegenerate E-space U and V=U perpendicular W. Let M_E be the projective unitary subgroup preserving this decomposition, and put D_E=N_Aut(S)(M_E).

For r in {p,q}, every alpha in Aut(S) of order dividing r is S-conjugate into D_E. The subgroup D_E is proper and maps onto Aut(S)/S. Consequently, for every t>=1, every order-r element of Aut(S) wr Sym(t) is S^t-conjugate into D_E wr Sym(t). Hence no group X with S^t<=X<=Aut(S) wr Sym(t) is invariably generated by elements of orders p and q.

## 1. Exhaustive automorphism form
The target range has n>=2RE>=360, so S is simple and its Hermitian polar space has rank at least three. We first justify that every abstract automorphism of S is induced by a semilinear unitary similitude.

Choose a maximal totally isotropic flag, let P be its stabilizer, and let U_0 be the unipotent radical of P. The Sylow defining-characteristic subgroups, their normalizers, and the minimal parabolic overgroups reconstruct the chamber system of the Hermitian polar space. An abstract automorphism of S therefore induces an automorphism of that polar space. In rank at least three, a polar-space automorphism is semilinear: after choosing a hyperbolic frame, incidence with the frame reconstructs the projective coordinate lines, addition, and multiplication, giving one field automorphism sigma of k and a sigma-semilinear map Z of V. Preservation of polar incidence says that h(Zv,Zw) and sigma(h(v,w)) have the same zero sets. Applying this to v, w, and v+w shows that their ratio is independent of v and w, so Z is a semilinear Hermitian similitude. An automorphism acting trivially on the chamber system lies in the core of P, which is trivial because S is simple. Thus every automorphism has the form [A]sigma with A in GU(V) and sigma in Aut(k).

There is no missing independent graph operation. With the standard Hermitian Gram matrix, a unitary matrix g satisfies bar(g)=(g^(-1))^T, so entrywise bar is the inverse-transpose graph automorphism on S. Since Aut(k) is cyclic of order 2f when Q=ell^f, graph and graph-field cosets are already among the sigma-components. In particular, a nontrivial prime-order sigma has order r; for odd r its fixed field retains a nontrivial Hermitian involution, while for r=2 it is the unique involution bar.

## 2. Bounded Hermitian self-dual blocks
Let A in GU(V) have multiplier mu and suppose A^r=lambda I. Then V is an orthogonal sum of nondegenerate A-invariant subspaces of dimensions at most 2r.

Indeed, regard V as a module over
R=k[T,T^(-1)]/(T^r-lambda),
with T acting as A. This finite principal ideal algebra has k-dimension r, and each indecomposable finite R-module is a cyclic primary quotient, hence has k-dimension at most r. The Hermitian adjoint relation is
h(Tv,w)=h(v,T-star w),  T-star=mu T^(-1),  c-star=bar(c).
This is compatible with T^r=lambda because mu^r=lambda bar(lambda). Thus h identifies V with its star-twisted dual.

Apply Krull-Schmidt to the R-module. Modulo the categorical radical, the Gram morphism of h is a nonsingular Hermitian matrix over a product of division rings. If a diagonal entry is a unit, one indecomposable summand is nondegenerate. Otherwise nonsingularity supplies a unit off-diagonal entry, and the corresponding two-by-two principal block is nonsingular modulo the radical. The radical is nilpotent, so this block lifts to a nondegenerate sum of two indecomposables. Its orthogonal complement is A-invariant because A is a similitude. Iterating gives the asserted orthogonal decomposition, with every block containing one or two indecomposables and therefore having dimension at most 2r. This argument also covers characteristic r, where T^r-lambda can be inseparable.

Let m_e be the number of blocks of dimension e. Every e<=2r divides E. If m_e<E/e for every e, then
n=sum_e e m_e < 2rE <= 2RE,
contrary to n>=n0. Hence E/e blocks of some common dimension e have a nondegenerate A-invariant sum U-prime of dimension E. Its orthogonal complement is also A-invariant. A unitary isometry taking U-prime perpendicular U-prime-perp to U perpendicular W puts the projective class of A in M_E. This proves the inner-diagonal case up to the PSU correction in Section 5.

## 3. Odd-order semilinear descent
Suppose alpha=[A]sigma and sigma has odd prime order r. Put K=k^sigma. From alpha^r=1 we have
A sigma(A) ... sigma^(r-1)(A)=lambda I
with lambda in K-star. Norm surjectivity supplies c in k-star with Norm_(k/K)(c)=lambda^(-1). Replacing A by B=cA does not change alpha and gives the exact cocycle
B sigma(B) ... sigma^(r-1)(B)=I.
Matrix Hilbert 90 gives X in GL(V) with B=X sigma(X)^(-1).

Let J be a sigma-fixed Hermitian Gram matrix and J_X=bar(X)^T J X. If nu is the multiplier of B, the similitude equation and the displayed cocycle identity give
J_X=nu sigma(J_X),  Norm_sigma(nu)=1.
Here nu belongs to F_Q-star. Since r is odd, sigma restricts to an automorphism of order r on F_Q. Scalar Hilbert 90 inside F_Q gives a in F_Q-star with sigma(a)=nu a. Therefore C=aJ_X is sigma-fixed. The involution bar restricts nontrivially to K, and C is a nondegenerate Hermitian form over K. All nondegenerate Hermitian forms of a fixed dimension over a finite field are isometric, so some Y in GL_n(K) satisfies bar(Y)^T C Y=J. For Z=XY we then have
B=Z sigma(Z)^(-1),  bar(Z)^T J Z=a^(-1)J.
Thus Z is a unitary similitude, and projective conjugation by Z sends alpha to the coordinate automorphism sigma. The latter preserves U perpendicular W and lies in D_E.

## 4. The graph involution by fixed-point descent
The preceding pure-field conjugacy argument must not be imposed when r=2, because sigma=bar and the involution on K=k^bar=F_Q becomes trivial. Instead let alpha=[A]bar. Projective order two gives A bar(A)=lambda I with lambda in F_Q-star. Choose c with c bar(c)=lambda^(-1), put B=cA, and let T=B bar. Then T^2=1. If nu is the multiplier of B, comparison of multipliers in B bar(B)=I gives nu^2=1.

Galois descent gives an n-dimensional K-space V_0=Fix(T) with V=k tensor_K V_0. For v,w in V_0,
h(v,w)=h(Tv,Tw)=nu bar(h(v,w)).
If nu=1, the restriction b=h|V_0 is a nondegenerate symmetric K-bilinear form. If nu=-1, necessarily the characteristic is odd; choose theta with bar(theta)=-theta and put b=theta^(-1)h|V_0. Then b is a nondegenerate alternating K-bilinear form. In characteristic two the first case may itself be alternating, which is harmless.

Because E is even, every such b has a nondegenerate E-dimensional subspace U_0. For an alternating form this follows from a symplectic basis. For a symmetric form in odd characteristic, use an orthogonal basis. In characteristic two, split nonsingular one-spaces until the remaining summand is alternating. If the alternating remainder has dimension at least E, choose E dimensions from it; otherwise the nonsingular one-space part has dimension greater than E because n>2E, so choose E of those lines. In every case the b-orthogonal complement is nondegenerate.

Set U-prime=k tensor_K U_0. It is T-invariant and h-nondegenerate, and U-prime-perp is T-invariant. A unitary isometry carrying this decomposition to U perpendicular W conjugates alpha into D_E. This handles every diagonal-graph involution. Since graph equals bar and all field operations form one cyclic group, it also exhausts the prime-order graph-field cases.

## 5. PSU correction and outer-coset realization
The preceding conjugators lie in PGU(V), whereas the theorem requires conjugation by S. The subgroup M_E maps onto PGU(V)/PSU(V): after scaling a similitude to an isometry, every determinant class is represented by an isometry acting on one nonsingular coordinate in U and trivially on the other coordinates. Given a projective unitary conjugator x, choose d in M_E with xS=dS and put s=xd^(-1) in S. Since d and the already normalized conjugate of alpha lie in D_E, alpha^s lies in D_E.

The same observation shows that D_E maps onto Aut(S)/S. Its block subgroup realizes every diagonal coset, while the coordinate field automorphisms realize every field, graph, and graph-field coset.

Because n>2E, the two summands have unequal dimensions. The normalizer of M_E preserves the E-dimensional summand, and S contains unitary root elements mixing U and W. Hence D_E is proper.

## 6. Wreath-product gauge
Let z=(g_1,...,g_t)pi in Aut(S) wr Sym(t) have order dividing the prime r. Every cycle of pi has length one or r. At a fixed point, the corresponding label has order dividing r and the local theorem moves it into D_E by an S-conjugation.

On an r-cycle, z^r=1 says that the ordered product of its edge labels is the identity in Aut(S). An Aut(S)-valued coordinate gauge therefore trivializes all labels on the cycle. For every gauge entry u_i choose d_i in D_E with u_iS=d_iS, possible because D_E maps onto Aut(S)/S, and put s_i=u_i d_i^(-1) in S. Replacing the u_i-gauge by the S-valued gauge s_i changes the trivial labels to products of elements of D_E, so all resulting labels lie in D_E. Treating cycles independently yields an S^t-conjugate of z in D_E wr Sym(t).

If S^t<=X<=Aut(S) wr Sym(t) and a,b in X have orders p and q, independently conjugate a and b by elements of S^t into D_E wr Sym(t). Both conjugates remain in X and lie in the proper subgroup X intersect (D_E wr Sym(t)), which cannot contain S^t. Thus they do not invariably generate X.

## Five-step proof spine
1. Intrinsic unitary building reconstruction gives alpha=[A]sigma and identifies graph with bar.
2. Twisted self-dual Krull-Schmidt gives bounded nondegenerate blocks for inner-diagonal elements.
3. Odd-order semilinear elements descend to a Hermitian form over k^sigma and are conjugate to coordinate sigma.
4. Graph involutions descend instead to symmetric or alternating fixed-point forms, which contain the required E-space.
5. PSU coset correction, properness, and the wreath gauge give the common proper overgroup theorem.

Existing verified PSL and PSp bridges together with this theorem exclude all high-dimensional linear, symplectic, and unitary chief factors. The single remaining theorem-level gap on this spine is the orthogonal-family outer-coset normalizer theorem together with its terminal bounded-rank CFSG assembly.

## Self-check
The bound is uniform in Q and handles defining characteristic. The module involution includes the Q-Frobenius twist. Odd-order descent uses that bar remains nontrivial on k^sigma. The unique order-two field operation is bar, so its fixed form is treated separately rather than incorrectly declared Hermitian. The selected E-space is nondegenerate and has an invariant orthogonal complement. Every projective conjugator is corrected inside the fixed decomposition subgroup, and the wreath-cycle label product is the identity in Aut(S), not merely modulo S. No assertion about the unresolved orthogonal family is used.
