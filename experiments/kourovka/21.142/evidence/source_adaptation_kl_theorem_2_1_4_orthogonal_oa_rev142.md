The exact source match is Kleidman–Liebeck, The Subgroup Structure of the Finite Classical Groups, Theorem 2.1.4, Chapter 2, Section 2.1, printed page 16. Section 2.1 first defines projectivization by quotienting scalar transformations and defines the semisimilarity group Γ(V,F,Q) through Q(vg)=lambda Q(v)^sigma. Theorem 2.1.4 identifies the source's full projective automorphism candidate with Aut(PΩ), except for even-characteristic PSp_4 and plus-type PΩ_8, where the indices are 2 and 3. In orthogonal type outside PΩ_8^+, that candidate is the projective semisimilarity image. For local B_m(Q), Q odd and m>=5, the dimension is 2m+1>=11. For local D_m^±(Q), m>=5, the dimension is 2m>=10. Hence the dimension condition holds, both signs and every characteristic in even dimension are covered, and the D_4 triality exception is impossible. Simplicity is an explicit local assumption, so the source's simplicity hypothesis is met and high rank excludes the small accidental cases. Odd-dimensional B type in characteristic two is deliberately outside this orthogonal application and is handled by the integrated symplectic family. Therefore every abstract automorphism in each local orthogonal family is induced by a projective semilinear similarity, so the existing semilinear decomposition-normalizer and wreath-gauge argument applies without rebuilding the polar-space geometry. As a proof-level supporting source, Steinberg, Automorphisms of Finite Linear Groups, Canadian Journal of Mathematics 12 (1960), Theorem 3.2 and Sections 5–7, DOI 10.4153/CJM-1960-054-6, proves that every automorphism of the finite groups of types B_m, D_m, and twisted 2D_m is a product of inner, diagonal, field, and graph automorphisms. Its normalization of opposite root subgroups and extraction of graph and field factors confirms that the cited equality concerns all abstract automorphisms, not merely a preselected geometric subgroup.
