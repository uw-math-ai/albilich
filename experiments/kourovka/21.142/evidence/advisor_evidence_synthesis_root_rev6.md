Decision: keep and repair the negative chief-factor obstruction trunk, and promote the residual classical common-overgroup theorem to the decisive root bottleneck. The alternating-chief-factor argument and the inner-diagonal PSL argument give a proof-shaped spine, whereas the bounded (2,3) sweep is only corroborating evidence and Teräväinen's theorem applies only when the two primes may vary with the degree.

The nearest full-proof shape is: if A_m embeds in a finite host H, then A_m embeds in a nonabelian simple constituent S of a chief factor of H; exclude large alternating constituents and all sufficiently high-rank classical constituents from groups invariably generated by fixed orders p and q; then use CFSG together with characteristic-uniform lower bounds for projective representations of A_m to bound m for every remaining simple family. Any larger A_m is then a counterexample to the root statement.

The residual theorem should not be attacked simultaneously for every classical family. First isolate the PSL outer-automorphism layer. For fixed p and q, prove or refute that, in sufficiently large dimension, every order-p or order-q element of Aut(PSL_n(F)) is PSL_n(F)-conjugate into the normalizer of a proper two-block decomposition of one universal bounded block dimension, with that normalizer realizing the required diagonal, field, graph, and graph-field outer cosets. The proof must include the corresponding S^t wreath-gauge statement. This is the smallest test that decides whether the inner-diagonal argument genuinely extends or whether outer automorphisms kill the route.

Do not infer a root counterexample from the alternating sweep alone. Do not begin the symplectic, unitary, and orthogonal families until the PSL outer-coset normalizer test has either been proved or explicitly refuted.
