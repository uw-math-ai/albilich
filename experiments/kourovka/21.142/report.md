## Final Proof

# A negative answer to Kourovka Notebook Problem 21.142

## Statement

Let $p$ and $q$ be distinct primes. There exists an integer $m \ge 9$ such that the alternating group $A_m$ does not embed in any finite group invariably generated by an element of order $p$ and an element of order $q$. Consequently, the answer to Kourovka Notebook Problem 21.142 is negative.

## Proof

We use the following two previously established and verified structural lemmas.

### Minimal-host lemma

Let $m \ge 5$. Suppose that a finite group containing $A_m$ is invariably generated by elements of orders $p$ and $q$, and choose such a group $G$ of minimum order. Then $G$ has a unique nonabelian minimal normal subgroup $N=S^t$, where $S$ is a nonabelian finite simple group, and conjugation identifies

\[
S^t \le G \le \operatorname{Aut}(S) \wr \operatorname{Sym}(t).
\]

Moreover, the embedded copy of $A_m$ lies in $N$, and one coordinate projection restricts to an injection $A_m \hookrightarrow S$. In particular, $S$ contains a subgroup isomorphic to $A_m$.

### Terminal simple-factor lemma

For the fixed primes $p$ and $q$, there is an integer $m \ge 9$ with the following property. Whenever a nonabelian finite simple group $S$ contains $A_m$, an integer $t \ge 1$ is given, and

\[
S^t \le X \le \operatorname{Aut}(S) \wr \operatorname{Sym}(t),
\]

then every element $x \in X$ of order $p$ and every element $y \in X$ of order $q$ admit independently chosen conjugators $u,v \in S^t$ and a common proper subgroup $D<X$ such that $x^u,y^v \in D$.

Choose $m$ supplied by the terminal simple-factor lemma. Suppose, for a contradiction, that $A_m$ embeds in a finite group invariably generated by elements $x$ and $y$ of respective orders $p$ and $q$. Among all such hosts choose one, denoted by $G$, of minimum order.

The minimal-host lemma supplies a nonabelian finite simple group $S$ and an integer $t \ge 1$ such that

\[
S^t \le G \le \operatorname{Aut}(S) \wr \operatorname{Sym}(t),
\]

and $S$ contains a copy of $A_m$. We may therefore apply the terminal simple-factor lemma with $X=G$. It gives elements $u,v \in S^t$ and a proper subgroup $D<G$ for which $x^u,y^v \in D$. Since $S^t \le G$, both $u$ and $v$ are legitimate conjugators in the definition of invariable generation. Nevertheless,

\[
\langle x^u,y^v\rangle \le D<G.
\]

This contradicts the assumption that $x$ and $y$ invariably generate $G$, which requires $\langle x^g,y^h\rangle=G$ for every pair $g,h \in G$.

Thus no finite group invariably generated by elements of orders $p$ and $q$ can contain $A_m$. Since $A_m$ is finite, it is a counterexample to the proposed universal embedding property. Hence the answer to the problem is no.
