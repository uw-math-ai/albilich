The selected route proves the affirmative root answer with no gap. Lemmas 1 and 2 correctly derive 3-closedness and two-orbit synchronization from a base of size at most two. The maximal-subgroup argument exhausts the proper maximal subgroups of GL(3,2): the two S4 classes and the C7:C3 class. Every nonmaximal stabilizer is reduced to a subgroup having a conjugate with trivial intersection; the order-three boundary case is also covered by Sylow conjugacy (equivalently, by containment up to conjugacy in an exhibited S3). Thus every corresponding coset action has base size at most two. The proofs that the seven-point point and line actions and the eight-point projective-line action are individually 3-closed are correct. The six unordered exceptional orbit pairs are exhaustive. In the mixed P-Q case, the pointwise stabilizer of two points is a normal Klein four subgroup L of the joining-line stabilizer; its two four-point orbits on Q have stabilizer exactly the canonical A4, forcing the residual element into every line stabilizer and hence to be trivial. Duality gives the P*-Q case. Therefore every diagonal action on G/H disjoint union G/K for proper H,K, including repeated pairs and H=1 or K=1, is 3-closed. The already verified two-orbit criterion then applies in the correct direction to every faithful finite G-set. Since PSL(2,7) is a nonabelian simple group of Lie type, it supplies the required example.
