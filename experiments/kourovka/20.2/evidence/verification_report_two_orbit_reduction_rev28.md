The selected route proves the exact biconditional with no gaps. Necessity holds because every proper-subgroup coset action of a finite simple group is faithful, hence so is each indicated two-orbit action. Conversely, a 3-closure element on an arbitrary faithful S-set preserves every point-orbit and restricts to the 3-closure on every union of point-orbits. Every nontrivial orbit is faithful by simplicity, while singleton orbits are fixed pointwise. If there is one nontrivial orbit, the double-copy lifting argument and the repeated-pair hypothesis show that its restriction is induced by an element of S. If there are at least two nontrivial orbits, pairwise 3-closedness supplies elements inducing the closure permutation on a fixed first orbit and each other orbit; faithfulness of the first orbit forces all these elements to coincide. These cases are exhaustive and assemble to prove total 3-closedness. The separate PSL(2,7) computation is explicitly presented only as a consequence and is not used in the criterion, so its unresolved root-level debt does not create a gap in this local claim.
